Project Overview

The Our Science in Visuals project supports members of the INITIATE PhD program in developing practical science communication skills through the creation of visual media based on their research.

The project began with a cohort-wide survey to identify which visual formats participants were most interested in learning. Three formats stood out: the poster, the graphical abstract, and the infographic. Based on these results, the team gathered free, curated resources, organized below as general resources and then by media format.

Cohort members are encouraged to develop a visual representation of their research project for the October 2026 workshop, with support, feedback, and guidance from the project team throughout. The project will culminate in an exhibition of all visual works at the next INITIATE workshop in Québec City, Canada, in 2026.

ROOM II

Poster

EXH. 01 — Poster

The most widely used format

Posters are one of the most widely used formats for communicating scientific research, especially at conferences, workshops, student showcases, and public engagement events. An effective poster should do more than display information: it should guide a viewer quickly toward the main message of the work.

ROOM III

Graphical Abstract

EXH. 02 — Graphical Abstract

The key idea, at a glance

Graphical abstracts are concise visual summaries of a research article or scientific project. They are especially useful when the goal is to communicate the main idea or key finding quickly. Many journals now encourage or require them; in broader science communication, they are also useful for websites, project summaries, and social media posts.

ROOM IV

Infographic

EXH. 03 — Infographic

Making a complex idea memorable

Infographics are highly effective for communicating scientific ideas to broader or non-specialist audiences. They are especially useful when the goal is to explain a topic, present a sequence of ideas, summarize evidence, or make a complex issue more approachable and memorable.
